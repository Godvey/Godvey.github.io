Research Infrastructure

The Low-altitude Optimal Robotics & Engineering (L-ORE) Lab focuses on the algorithmic optimization and engineering implementation of low-altitude systems. Our facility features a 50-square-meter indoor flight arena integrated with a high-precision motion capture system to support multi-agent coordination. The lab maintains a diverse fleet of approximately 20 UAVs, including fully programmable Crazyflie drones, semi-programmable DJI Tello platforms, and various professional DJI consumer-grade drones. Furthermore, our infrastructure extends to specialized multi-rotor (quadrotors/hexarotors) and fixed-wing aircraft. These integrated resources provide a robust foundation for swarm intelligence experiments, with future expansions planned for large-scale outdoor flight missions.
