Research Infrastructure

The Low-altitude Optimal Robotics and Engineering (L-ORE) Laboratory focuses on the algorithmic optimization and engineering implementation of intelligent autonomous systems. Our facility features a 50 m² indoor flight arena integrated with a high-precision motion capture system to support complex multi-agent coordination experiments. The lab maintains a heterogeneous fleet of approximately 20 UAVs, including fully programmable Crazyflie nano-quadrotors, DJI Tello platforms, and professional DJI consumer-grade drones. Our resources further extend to customized hexarotors and fixed-wing aircraft, providing a robust foundation for swarm intelligence research and future large-scale outdoor missions.
